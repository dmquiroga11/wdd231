Natural Regions

Amazon Region

The Amazon region covers 42% of the national territory and is known as the lung of the world due to its vast jungle. It is home to the largest variety of freshwater fish on the planet and a variety of exotic mammals such as the pink river dolphin, the manatee, the jaguar, the puma, the giant otter, the peccary, the tapir, the capybara, the sloth and various primates. The largest river turtle and alligator in the world are also found there. It is made up of the Andes mountain range and plains that extend eastwards to the borders with Brazil, Peru, Ecuador and Venezuela. It shares with these countries the Amazon rainforest, which has one of the greatest biodiversity on the planet.

Andean Region

The Andean Region, one of the six natural regions of Colombia, is located in the center of the country and has a wide range of thermal floors and the largest number of natural parks. Three Andean mountain ranges, the western, central and eastern, pass through this welcoming region of Colombia. The geographic diversity is immense: the climate, the vegetation, the fauna, the bodies of water (the main rivers, Cauca and Magdalena), the existing types of relief (valleys, canyons, plateaus) and landscapes, among others.

Caribbean Region

The Caribbean region is one of the main tourist destinations in Colombia, located in the far north of the country. The topography of this entire region is very diverse, with seven national natural parks and four wildlife sanctuaries. When you go to this region, you can see the dry forest ecosystems in La Guajira; the tropical rainforest in the Gulf of Urabá; and the moorland in the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta, where the highest mountains in Colombia are located (Cristóbal Colón and Simón Bolívar peaks).

Pacific Region

It is located in western Colombia; it extends from the border with Ecuador to the border with Panama, and from the crest of the Western Cordillera to the Pacific coast. Thanks to the national natural parks (Ensenada de Utría, Gorgona, Tatamá, Los Katíos) and various ecological spaces in this region, many species of flora and fauna are preserved; as occurs in the Darién jungle. The climate is predominantly warm and humid with a high rainfall density, especially in Chocó, where the Atrato River is located, one of the most voluminous in the country. In addition to this, the San Juan, Baudó, Mira and Patía rivers constitute fundamental communication routes for the survival and progress of the region.

Orinoquia Region

This region is located in the east of the country. It is also known as the Eastern Plains. It is a region suitable for extensive livestock farming and agriculture because the territory is characterized by steppes, extensive savannahs and gallery forests (tall trees of different species along the rivers). The unique plains culture of the Orinoquia is characterized by the spirit and daily activities of the llanero (person who is dedicated to raising livestock), the spectacular landscapes, typical dances, the composition and interpretation of romantic songs, gastronomy and myths and legends. You can get to know this set of cultural expressions at any of the festivals that are traditionally held there.

Insular Region

All the islands that are far from the Caribbean and Pacific coasts of Colombia make up the insular region. The main economic activities of this region are tourism and fishing. Without a doubt, any of the beautiful islands that make up this region will seem like a peaceful place for a profitable rest. In general, they all have tropical aquatic paradises, fantastic beaches, beautiful ecological environments and waters suitable for practicing different water sports, including diving.
